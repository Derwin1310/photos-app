# Product

## Register

product

## Users

PicXplorer is for casual photo explorers who want a simple native app for searching themed photo collections and saving personal captures. They use it in short sessions, often on a phone, expecting quick browsing, clear navigation, and camera/gallery flows that feel familiar on iOS and Android.

## Product Purpose

PicXplorer helps users find visual inspiration through collections and keep their own captured moments in a local gallery. Success means users can search, browse, capture, caption, save, edit, share, and delete photos without friction or confusing navigation.

## Brand Personality

Vibrant, native, image-forward. The app should feel modern and polished without becoming loud, gimmicky, or detached from standard mobile patterns. Photography carries the emotion; the interface should frame it with confidence.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic AI-app sameness, over-decorated glass effects, handwritten body typography, decorative animations that slow the task, and inconsistent controls across screens. Avoid turning every surface into a card stack when a simpler native layout works better.

## Design Principles

- Keep photos first: UI should support discovery and saving without competing with imagery.
- Feel native before feeling novel: standard mobile affordances are preferred unless a custom pattern clearly improves the workflow.
- Make actions obvious: search, capture, save, edit, share, delete, and theme settings should be easy to find and hard to misread.
- Use vibrancy with restraint: color should signal brand, hierarchy, state, and selection rather than decorate every surface.
- Preserve momentum: loading, empty, error, and permission states should explain the next step quickly.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target accessible contrast for all text and controls in light and dark themes. Respect reduced-motion preferences where animations are decorative or feedback-only. Keep touch targets comfortable, labels descriptive, and state changes understandable without relying on color alone.
